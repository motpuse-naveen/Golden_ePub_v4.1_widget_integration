Challenge Yourself

Answer each question with a word, phrase, sentence, or numerical solution. When a list of possible answers is supplied with the question, one, more than one, or none of the choices may be correct. Correct answers are provided at the end of the book.

  1. 1.

    What are the units of hemodynamic resistance?

    Answer:
    mm Hg/mL/min or mm Hg/L/min
  2. 2.

    If heart rate is 75 beats/min, what is the R-R interval in units of milliseconds?
    (Hint: 60 s in a minute)

    Answer:
    800 ms
  3. 3.

    What is the correct order of the following events: Ca2+ binding to troponin C, tension, Ca2+ release from sarcoplasmic reticulum, ventricular action potential, Ca2+ accumulation by sarcoplasmic reticulum?

    Answer:
    Ventricular action potential; Ca2+ release from sarcoplasmic reticulum; Ca2+ binding to troponin C; tension; Ca2+ accumulation by sarcoplasmic reticulum
  4. 4.

    If heart rate is 85 beats/min, end-diastolic volume is 150 mL, and stroke volume is 75 mL, what is the ejection fraction?

    (Hint: Heart rate is not needed for the calculation.)

    Answer:
    0.50
  5. 5.

    Which portion of the cardiac cycle has the lower ventricular volume: atrial systole or isovolumetric ventricular relaxation?

    (Hint: Ventricle is filling during atrial systole.)

    Answer:
    Isovolumetric relaxation
  6. 6.

    According to the cardiac and vascular function curves, an increase in blood volume leads to _______ right atrial pressure and _______ cardiac output.

    Answer:
    Increased; increased
  7. 7.

    If cardiac output is 5.2 L/min, heart rate is 76 beats/min, and end-diastolic volume is 145 mL, what is the end-systolic volume?

    (Hint: First, calculate stroke volume from cardiac output and heart rate; then use calculated stroke volume and stated end-diastolic volume to calculate end-systolic volume.)

    Answer:
    77 mL
  8. 8.

    In a capillary, if Pc is 35 mm Hg, πc is 25 mm Hg, Pi is 2 mm Hg, and πi is 1 mm Hg, is there net absorption or filtration, and what is the magnitude of the driving force?

    Answer:
    Net filtration; driving force is 9 mm Hg
  9. 9.

    When a person moves quickly from a lying to a standing position, which of the following decrease(s)?

    1. 1.

      venous return

    2. 2.

      cardiac output

    3. 3.

      arterial pressure (Pa)

    Answer:
    venous return

    cardiac output

    arterial pressure (Pa)
  10. 10.

    What is the name of the volume contained in the left ventricle immediately before it contracts?

    Answer:
    End-diastolic volume (or preload)
  11. 11.

    Which of the following produce(s) an increase in contractility?

    1. decreased heart rate

    2. increased phosphorylation of phospholamban

    3. increased action potential duration

    Answer:
    increased phosphorylation of phospholamban

    increased action potential duration
  12. 12.

    During which phase of the ventricular action potential, phase 0 or phase 4, is inward current greater than outward current?

    Answer:
    Phase 0
  13. 13.

    Which term best applies to the ARP of the ventricular action potential?

    1. automaticity

    2. excitability

    3. conduction velocity

    4. maximum diastolic potential

    Answer:
    excitability
  14. 14.

    If, simultaneously, there is an increased rate of phase 4 depolarization and hyperpolarization of the threshold potential, will there be an increase, decrease, or no change in heart rate?

    (Hint: Each change, by itself, would lead to increased heart rate.)

    Answer:
    Increased heart rate
  15. 15.

    Among the responses that occur following hemorrhage, which of the following increase(s)?

    (Hint: Unstressed volume would decrease due to venoconstriction.)

    1. unstressed volume

    2. heart rate

    3. resistance of cutaneous vascular beds

    4. firing rate of carotid sinus nerves

    5. angiotensin II levels

    Answer:
    heart rate

    resistance of cutaneous vascular beds

    angiotensin II levels
  16. 16.

    In the myogenic mechanism of autoregulation, according to the law of Laplace, does an increase in pressure lead to an increase, decrease, or no change in the radius of the blood vessel?

    (Hint: T = P × r. Thus if P increases, r must decrease to maintain constant wall tension.)

    Answer:
    Decreased radius
  17. 17.

    Of the following, which circulation receives the highest percentage of the cardiac output?

    (Hint: Pulmonary blood flow is 100% of cardiac output.)

    1. renal

    2. pulmonary

    3. coronary

    4. skeletal muscle during intense exercise

    5. skin during intense exercise

    Answer:
    pulmonary
  18. 18.

    Which of the following cause(s) an increase in stroke volume from the left ventricle?

    (Hints: End-diastolic volume is preload, and aortic pressure is afterload.)

    1. increased contractility

    2. decrease in end-diastolic volume

    3. increase in aortic pressure

    Answer:
    increased contractility
  19. 19.

    During which portion(s) of the cardiac cycle is the aortic valve open?

    1. atrial systole

    2. rapid ventricular ejection

    3. diastasis

    Answer:
    rapid ventricular ejection
  20. 20.

    According to the cardiac and vascular function curves, an increase in TPR leads to _______ venous return and _______ cardiac output.

    Answer:
    Decreased; decreased
  21. 21.

    Which situation is associated with the higher rate of myocardial oxygen consumption: increased cardiac output secondary to increased heart rate or decreased cardiac output secondary to increased aortic pressure?

    (Hint: Pressure work is more costly than volume work.)

    Answer:
    Decreased cardiac output caused by increased aortic pressure
  22. 22.

    Three resistors, each with a value of 10, are arranged in parallel. How much does total resistance change if a fourth resistor with a value of 10 is added in parallel?

    Answer:
    Total resistance decreases from 3.33 to 2.5.
  23. 23.

    Blood vessel “A” has a cross-sectional area of 1 cm2, and blood vessel “B” has a cross-sectional area of 10 cm2. If blood flow through the two vessels is the same, in which vessel is velocity of blood flow higher?

    (Hint: Velocity = Flow/Area.)

    Answer:
    Blood vessel “A”
  24. 24.

    Where am I? For each item in the following list, give its correct location in the cardiovascular system. The location may be anatomic, a graph or portion of a graph, an equation, or a concept.
    Dicrotic notch
    β1 receptors
    Lmax
    Radius to the fourth power
    Phospholamban
    Negative dromotropic effect
    Pulse pressure
    Normal automaticity
    Ejection fraction

    Answer:
    Dicrotic notch: arterial pressure trace

    β1 receptors: sinoatrial node and ventricular muscle
    Lmax: length-tension curve
    Radius to the fourth power: resistance of blood vessels or resistance equation
    Phospholamban: sarcoplasmic reticulum
    Negative dromotropic effect: AV node
    Pulse pressure: arteries or arterial pressure
    Normal automaticity: SA node
    Ejection fraction: ventricle
  25. 25.

    During which portions(s) of the cardiac cycle is the mitral valve closed: atrial systole, rapid ventricular ejection, isovolumetric ventricular relaxation, diastasis?

    Answer:
    Rapid ventricular ejection, isovolumetric ventricular relaxation
  26. 26.

    During exercise, which of the following decrease(s): heart rate, venous return, stroke volume, diameter of splanchnic arterioles, TPR?

    Answer:
    Diameter of splanchnic arterioles, TPR
  27. 27.

    According to the ventricular pressure-volume loop, an increase in afterload produces an increase in which of the following?

    1. 1.

      end-diastolic volume

    2. 2.

      end-diastolic pressure

    3. 3.

      end-systolic volume

    4. 4.

      stroke volume

    Answer:

  28. 28.

    Which of the following is/are mediated by an increase in ICa?

    1. 1.

      sympathetic effect to increase heart rate

    2. 2.

      parasympathetic effect to decrease heart rate

    3. 3.

      sympathetic effect to increase contractility

    4. 4.

      parasympathetic effect to decrease conduction velocity in AV node

  29. 29.

    A sudden decrease in ventricular contractility causes a decrease in stroke volume from 70 mL to 60 mL. Following Frank-Starling compensation for the decrease in contractility, what is the stroke volume?

    (Hint: Following a sudden decrease in contractility, Frank-Starling compensation partially corrects stroke volume toward normal.)

    1. 1.

      75 mL

    2. 2.

      70 mL

    3. 3.

      65 mL

    4. 4.

      60 mL

    5. 5.

      55 mL

  30. 30.

    A third-year medical student is on his first day in the operating room. He is standing near the surgical field and starts to feel lightheaded. The next thing he knows, he is lying on the floor and regaining consciousness. Which of the following events caused him to faint

    1. 1.

      increased sympathetic activity to the SA node

    2. 2.

      increased sympathetic activity to α1 receptors on splanchnic arterioles

    3. 3.

      decreased parasympathetic activity to the SA node

      decreased cardiac output?
